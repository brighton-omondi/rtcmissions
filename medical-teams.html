Medical Missions


Become a part of us:

Most people in developed parts of the world spend their days listening to only one “FM” station in their minds. That “station” is WIIFM, which stands for What’s In It for Me! Our world would be a better place if we all just changed that questions to “How Can I Help?”

Each year Medical Mission Service Trips are scheduled to provide an opportunity for medical professionals and other volunteer to participate in short-term mission teams work at our RTC Medical Centre campus in the Southwestern part of Kenya near Lake Victoria and Oyugis, Kenya. RTC Missions is currently planning Annual medical mission trips. You can learn more about these upcoming trips by contacting info@rtchministries.org. One team may include many different areas of medical expertise and will serve a variety of people in the Bondo Awino village who need a broad scope of medical services.

Ways to Help

When you become a member of RTC family, you will receive news and information about RTC Missions and discover new opportunities for involvement with this renowned and respected work. Please see the following examples…

Pray

You can pray for the missionaries, staff, operations and specific projects and needs we will bring to your attention.

Be an ambassador

You can be an ambassador of RTC Missions to your Church’s mission abroad, promoting prayer and support for the missions’ work

Encourage volunteer

You can encourage colleagues to consider a short-term, mid-term or even long-term mission assignment at the RTC Medical Centre in Southwestern Kenya.

Service

You can serve at RTC Medical Centre through short-term, mid-term or long-term medical mission assignment in your area of expertise

Give

You can give a tax deductible financial gift to meet critical needs at RTC Missions. Please visit our Donate page to choose your option


Short Term Medical Missionary Service:

Missionary teams represent many different specialties that RTC Medical Centre is prepared to receive. These most common specialists include ENT, OB/GYN, Anesthesiology, Gastroenterology, General Surgery, Pediatric Surgery and Orthopedic Surgery. All other medical professionals are welcome and encouraged to consider a mission trip which is life-changing for both the care givers and the patients alike.

Join with us on a 12-day short-term medical missions trip and help us save lives and provide “Maisha Bora” (Swahili for “better life”) for your brothers and sisters in Africa. You are desperately needed. To begin the process and decide if this type of missionary service is right for you, complete and submit the form below to our Medical Director. Thank you!

The typical OUT-OF-POCKET COSTS for a medical missionary team member:


Round Trip Plane Ticket prices vary depending on the time of the year, class of service you choose and how far in advance you purchase your ticket.


We recommend $100-200 cash needed for when you get to Kenya (will be exchanged for Kenyan Shillings early after arrival).


Additional cash amount of your discretion for souvenirs. (We suggest $100-200. Many vendors take credit cards also)


Additional expenses that may apply to you are, U.S. passport fee, Kenyan eVisa and immunizations, if you choose to take some.


Licensed physicians will need to apply for a temporary license with the Ministry of Health The fee is $200. This temporary license can be obtained through RTC Missions, Inc. Nurses do not require licensing


Is a VISA required to enter Kenya and how do I acquire one?

A Kenyan VISA is required. The easiest method for acquiring a Kenyan VISA is with an official eVISA which can be applied for here immigration.ecitizen.go.ke

What immunizations will I need?

Check with your personal doctor, a local travel clinic, or health department as soon as you have chosen to take part in a trip to Kenya to ensure you receive all the proper immunizations and medications at least 2 weeks before you travel.


Recommended for trips with RTC Missions, Inc.

  • Hepatitis A&B (Non-Medical Teams do not require Hep B)
  • Typhoid
  • Tetanus (if not current)
  • ANTI-MALARIAL prescription (Suggested: Malarone, Larium, or Doxycycline)
  • Broad-spectrum antibiotic such as Cipro in case needed while traveling

    • Recommended immunization by the CDC for travel to Kenya

  • Yellow Fever (not as prevalent in our area)
